- Hair thinning (recent)
- Remote Graves' disease/orbitopathy
- Breast cancer history (ER/PR+, on tamoxifen)
- Jaw pain episode

- ANA positive 1:640 (homogeneous)
- Anti-SSA antibody elevated (80.76 U)
- **IgG4 elevated (137.3 mg/dl, reference <86.4)**
- Normal inflammatory markers (ESR 6, CRP 0.6)
- Normal CSF
- Negative temporal artery ultrasound

## Analysis of Key Findings

**The orbital pathology pattern is crucial:** Enhancement involves the optic nerve sheath and intraconal fat with relative sparing of the optic nerve itself. This is characteristic of IgG4-related disease rather than optic neuritis.

**The elevated IgG4** strongly supports IgG4-related disease, which can cause:
- Orbital pseudotumor
- Lacrimal gland involvement
- Cranial nerve palsies
- TMJ involvement

**The anti-SSA positivity and ANA** could suggest Sjögren syndrome, but the patient lacks sicca symptoms and has normal salivary function.

**Normal inflammatory markers** argue against active vasculitis (like GCA) and most infectious etiologies.

## Differential Diagnosis (Ranked by Probability)

1. **IgG4-Related Ophthalmic Disease** - Elevated IgG4, orbital sheath/fat involvement pattern, TMJ involvement, relative nerve sparing
   
2. **Sjögren Syndrome with Neurologic Involvement** - Elevated anti-SSA, ANA positive, but lacks sicca symptoms

3. **Systemic Lupus Erythematosus** - ANA positive, photosensitivity, but anti-dsDNA negative, no other organ involvement

4. **Sarcoidosis** - Can cause orbital disease and cranial neuropathies, but no lymphadenopathy, normal ACE

5. **Idiopathic Orbital Inflammatory Disease (Orbital Pseudotumor)** - Pattern fits, but elevated IgG4 suggests specific etiology

## Reasoning for Leading Diagnosis

**IgG4-Related Ophthalmic Disease** best explains:

1. **Imaging pattern:** Perineural/perivascular enhancement with relative nerve sparing is classic for IgG4-RD
2. **Elevated serum IgG4** (137.3 mg/dl, >1.5x upper limit)
3. **Sequential orbital involvement** - IgG4-RD commonly affects orbits bilaterally
4. **TMJ involvement** - IgG4-RD can involve multiple sites
5. **Normal inflammatory markers** - typical for IgG4-RD
6. **Skin biopsy showing eosinophils** - consistent with IgG4-RD histology
7. **Association with autoimmune thyroid disease** (Graves') - known association
8. **Response to corticosteroids** - characteristic of IgG4-RD

The anti-SSA positivity may represent an overlapping autoimmune diathesis or false positive, but the clinical syndrome and IgG4 elevation point more strongly to IgG4-related disease.
