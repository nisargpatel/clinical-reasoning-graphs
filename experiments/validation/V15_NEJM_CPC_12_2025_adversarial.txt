## Phase 2: ADVERSARIAL SELF-CRITIQUE

### Strongest Argument AGAINST Scabies:

The **facial involvement** is the most problematic feature. Classic scabies typically **spares the face** in immunocompetent adults. While crusted (Norwegian) scabies can involve the face, this occurs primarily in immunocompromised patients (HIV, organ transplant, chronic steroids, HTLV-1). This patient has no documented immunocompromise.

### Unexplained Findings:

1. **Oral ulcer on anterior upper gum**: Not explained by scabies
2. **Facial rash (forehead, nose, cheeks, chin)**: Unusual distribution for scabies
3. **Atypical lymphocytes**: While possible with scabies, this is more classically associated with viral infections (EBV, CMV, HIV)
4. **The constellation of oral ulcer + facial rash + palmar rash**: This triad is more suggestive of secondary syphilis

### Alternative Diagnosis:

**Secondary Syphilis** could better explain the FULL constellation:
- **Palmar involvement**: Classic for secondary syphilis
- **Facial rash**: Common in secondary syphilis
- **Oral ulceration (mucous patch)**: Pathognomonic for secondary syphilis
- **Diffuse papular rash**: Characteristic
- **Mild systemic symptoms**: Compatible
- **Atypical lymphocytes**: Can occur in syphilis
- **Risk factors**: Homelessness is associated with higher STI rates, though she denies sexual activity

### "Can't Miss" Diagnosis:

**Secondary syphilis** is the critical "can't miss" diagnosis. The oral ulcer is a red flag that I may have underweighted. Mucous patches (shallow oral ulcers) are highly specific for secondary syphilis. The denial of sexual activity doesn't exclude syphilis (recall bias, sexual assault, sharing needles for drug use despite denial).
