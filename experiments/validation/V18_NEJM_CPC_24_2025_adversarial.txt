## Phase 1 — Initial Assessment

### Key Clinical Features
- 32-year-old woman with recent COVID-19 reinfection (5 weeks prior)
- Progressive symptoms: neck pain → fatigue → myalgias → chest pain (pleuritic/positional) → palpitations
- **Cardiac conduction abnormalities**: First-degree AV block progressing to Mobitz type I second-degree AV block, bradycardia (HR 50s), irregularly irregular rhythm
- **Q waves in inferior leads** - concerning for myocardial involvement
- Elevated BNP (604 pg/mL), transaminases elevated then improving
- Elevated D-dimer (1760 → 436)
- Lives in forested area in New England with animal exposure (rabbits, sheep)
- Unvaccinated against COVID-19

### Initial Differential Diagnosis

1. **Lyme Carditis** (40%) - Lives in endemic area (forested New England), progressive AV block (hallmark of Lyme carditis), young healthy patient, recent outdoor activities. The progression from first-degree to Mobitz I is classic. Initial negative Borrelia testing was 2.5 years ago.

2. **Viral/Post-COVID Myocarditis** (30%) - Recent COVID reinfection, chest pain (pleuritic/positional), elevated BNP, conduction abnormalities, Q waves could represent myocardial inflammation/injury.

3. **Acute Rheumatic Fever** (10%) - Carditis with conduction abnormalities, myalgias, but no preceding streptococcal infection documented, no other Jones criteria.

4. **Cardiac Sarcoidosis** (10%) - AV block in young patient, could explain multi-system symptoms, but no lymphadenopathy on imaging.

5. **Tick-borne coinfection (Anaplasmosis/Ehrlichiosis)** (10%) - Endemic area, elevated transaminases, myalgias, but typically presents with more acute febrile illness.

### Leading Diagnosis
**Lyme Carditis** - Probability: 40%

**Reasoning**: The constellation of progressive AV nodal block (first-degree → Mobitz I) in a young, otherwise healthy woman living in a Lyme-endemic area is highly characteristic. The pattern of AV block fluctuation is typical. Initial Lyme testing was 2.5 years ago and wouldn't reflect recent infection.

## Phase 2 — Adversarial Self-Critique

### Arguments AGAINST Lyme Carditis:
- No documented erythema migrans rash
- No documented recent tick bite
- Previous Lyme testing was negative (though years ago)
- The chest pain being pleuritic/positional is more suggestive of pericarditis than isolated conduction system disease
- Q waves in inferior leads suggest actual myocardial injury, which is less common in Lyme carditis (primarily affects conduction system)

### Findings NOT explained by Lyme Carditis:
- The elevated BNP suggests ventricular dysfunction, uncommon in typical Lyme carditis
- Q waves suggest myocardial damage beyond conduction system
- The prominent pleuritic/positional chest pain suggests pericardial involvement
- The gastrointestinal symptoms (months of abdominal discomfort, nausea, diarrhea)

### Alternative diagnosis that could explain findings better:
**Viral/Post-COVID Myopericarditis** - Would explain:
- Pleuritic/positional chest pain (pericarditis component)
- Conduction abnormalities and Q waves (myocarditis)
- Elevated BNP (ventricular dysfunction)
- Timeline following COVID-19 reinfection
- Myalgias and systemic symptoms

### "Can't Miss" Diagnosis:
- **Giant cell myocarditis** - rapidly progressive, high mortality, can present with heart block
- **Cardiac sarcoidosis** - AV block is often first manifestation

My self-critique has **modified but not completely changed** my assessment. The presence of Q waves and elevated BNP does suggest myocardial involvement beyond pure conduction system disease.

However, I maintain **Lyme Carditis** as my leading diagnosis because:
1. The geographic and exposure history is compelling
