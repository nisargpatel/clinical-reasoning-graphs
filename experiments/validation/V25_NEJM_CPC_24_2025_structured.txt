- **Temporal fit**: While the post-COVID timing (5 weeks) is plausible, the absence of acute cardiac symptoms immediately following COVID and the later onset argues against direct viral myocarditis
- **Other findings**: The transaminitis could occur with either. The BNP elevation is mild and nonspecific.

**Unexplained evidence assessment:**
- The chronic GI symptoms are likely unrelated to the acute presentation (possibly related to her chronic gastritis and herbal supplement use)
- The mechanical trigger (neck strain) may be coincidental timing
- The absence of documented rash/tick bite is not unexpected (many Lyme carditis patients don't recall these)
- Previous negative serology is from a different time period and clinical context

The unexplained evidence is not clinically significant enough to change my ranking. The **progressive AV block pattern in an endemic area is highly specific for Lyme carditis**.
