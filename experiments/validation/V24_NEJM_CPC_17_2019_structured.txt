## Phase 1 — INITIAL ASSESSMENT

### A. PROBLEM REPRESENTATION

A 44-year-old man from sub-Saharan Africa with chronic progressive polyarticular inflammatory arthritis, profound cachexia with 18kg weight loss, pancytopenia (severe microcytic anemia, leukopenia), large hemorrhagic pericardial effusion with tamponade, serositis (pleural effusions, ascites), generalized lymphadenopathy, and markedly elevated inflammatory markers.

### B. INITIAL DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS

1. **Disseminated Tuberculosis (TB)** (40%)
   - Most likely explanation given epidemiologic risk (sub-Saharan African origin), chronic systemic illness, serositis (pericardial, pleural, peritoneal), lymphadenopathy, profound constitutional symptoms, and cytopenias

2. **Adult-onset Still's Disease (AOSD)** (25%)
   - Explains polyarthritis, high fevers, serositis, marked leukopenia (paradoxical for classic AOSD but can occur), extremely high ferritin, lymphadenopathy

3. **Systemic Lupus Erythematosus (SLE)** (15%)
   - Accounts for polyarthritis, serositis (pleural, pericardial, peritoneal), cytopenias, rash, constitutional symptoms, proteinuria

4. **Lymphoma (T-cell or Hodgkin)** (10%)
   - Explains cachexia, lymphadenopathy, B symptoms, cytopenias, serositis from lymphatic obstruction

5. **HIV-associated disease** (10%)
   - Despite negative screening, explains pancytopenia, wasting, opportunistic infections, lymphadenopathy given sexual activity and endemic area origin

**Leading Diagnosis: Disseminated Tuberculosis - 40%**

**Reasoning:** The combination of origin from TB-endemic region, chronic progressive course over 10 months, profound wasting, pericardial effusion (TB is the leading cause of pericarditis in endemic areas), serositis involving multiple serous cavities, lymphadenopathy, and cytopenias strongly suggests disseminated TB. The hemorrhagic pericardial fluid with high protein and neutrophilic predominance is consistent with tuberculous pericarditis.

## Phase 2 — STRUCTURED SECOND LOOK

### A. PROBLEM REPRESENTATION CHECK

Re-examining my representation: I captured the key features, but I should emphasize:
- **Hyperacute deterioration** in final weeks superimposed on chronic illness
- **Extremely high ferritin** (1,921 μg/L) with very low iron and TIBC (anemia of chronic disease)
- **Profound leukopenia** (not typical for most infections)
- **Negative interferon-gamma release assay (IGRA)** for TB - this is critical evidence AGAINST TB
- The **polyarthritis is TRUE synovitis** (not just arthralgias) - warm knees with effusions, wrist and PIP involvement

### B. STRESS TEST LEADING DIAGNOSIS (TB)

**Strongest arguments AGAINST disseminated TB:**
1. **Negative IGRA** - highly sensitive for TB, especially in disseminated disease; negative test significantly reduces TB probability
2. **Profound leukopenia** - unusual for TB, which typically causes leukocytosis or normal counts
3. **True inflammatory polyarthritis** - not a typical feature of TB (Poncet's disease is rare, reactive, and non-erosive)
4. **Pericardial fluid negative for organisms** and predominantly neutrophilic rather than lymphocytic (TB usually lymphocyte-predominant)

**Unexplained findings:**
- Marked leukopenia with profound anemia
- True synovitis with knee effusions
- Extremely elevated ferritin

### C. CONSIDER ALTERNATIVES

**Best alternative: Adult-onset Still's Disease (AOSD)**

**Specific findings supporting AOSD over TB:**
1. **Extremely high ferritin** (1,921 μg/L) - hallmark of AOSD; ferritin >1,000 with low glycosylated fraction is highly specific
2. **High spiking fevers** (to 39.7°C) - characteristic quotidian fever pattern of AOSD
3. **True inflammatory polyarthritis** - cardinal feature of AOSD
4. **Serositis** (pericarditis, pleuritis, peritonitis) - classic for AOSD
5. **Lymphadenopathy** and hepatosplenomegaly (elevated alkaline phosphatase, AST/ALT) - typical
6. **Marked elevation of inflammatory markers** (ESR 105, CRP 86)
7. **Leukopenia can occur** in AOSD, though leukocytosis is more common
8. **Rash** - described as pruritic with scaling; salmon-pink evanescent rash is classic but not always present

**Can't-miss diagnosis: SLE**
- Also explains serositis, cytopenias, arthritis, constitutional symptoms
- But: no photosensitivity, no mention of ANA testing, rash doesn't fit malar or discoid pattern

### D. DEFEND OR UPDATE

**Findings MOST STRONGLY SUPPORTING AOSD:**
1. Ferritin >1,900 - extraordinarily high, nearly pathognomonic when >1,000
2. Quintuple serositis (pericardial, pleural, peritoneal)
3. True inflammatory arthritis with synovitis
4. Quotidian fevers
5. Lymphadenopathy and hepatic involvement
6. Marked systemic inflammation

**Comparison to TB:**
- **Base rates:** In a patient from TB-endemic area, TB has high prior probability, BUT
- **IGRA negative** dramatically reduces TB likelihood (sensitivity >95% in disseminated disease)
- **Clinical fit:** AOSD explains ALL findings including the profound leukopenia and extremely high ferritin
- TB does NOT explain the true polyarticular synovitis or extreme ferritin elevation

**Is unexplained evidence significant?**
- For TB: The negative IGRA, leukopenia, and true arthritis are clinically significant departures
- For AOSD: Leukopenia is unusual but can occur; all other findings fit perfectly

The ferritin level is the most discriminating finding - this degree of hyperferritinemia is exceptional and virtually diagnostic of AOSD in the right clinical context.
