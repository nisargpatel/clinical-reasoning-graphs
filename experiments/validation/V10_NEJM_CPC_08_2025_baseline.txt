### Step 5 — Account for the evolving high–anion gap metabolic acidosis (rank #4 and #5)

Why not ranked above #1–#3: the patient’s *presentation* and exam track best with CO₂ narcosis, and ketonuria appears later; also lactate is normal and there is significant renal dysfunction that can also contribute to AG elevation.

Rank #5 (AKI/uremia and mixed metabolic derangements):

- Potential causes: hypoperfusion/shock, cardiorenal syndrome/RV failure, nephrotoxic/diuretic effects, and possible postrenal obstruction from clots/tumor (though high urine output after diuretics argues against complete obstruction).
Why not higher: uremia may contribute, but the **degree of hypercapnia** is a more direct and sufficient explanation for the acute mental status change.
