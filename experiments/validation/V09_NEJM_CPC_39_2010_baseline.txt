However, fulminant Wilson disease characteristically features an AST > ALT, a markedly low alkaline phosphatase, and a Coombs-negative hemolytic anemia. Our patient has an ALT > AST, normal alkaline phosphatase, and her CBC is perfectly normal, ruling against Wilson.

    *   **Postpartum Timing:** This is the most critical clinical clue. The patient gave birth 3 months ago. The postpartum state is a classically recognized trigger for the de novo presentation or sudden flare of autoimmune diseases (like AIH and thyroiditis). This happens due to the abrupt loss of the immunotolerant state of pregnancy, leading to immune reconstitution and hyper-reactivity.
    *   **Autoantibodies:** The patient tested positive for Anti-Smooth Muscle Antibodies (ASMA) at a titer of 1:80 and ANA at 1:40. In evaluating adult AIH, an ASMA titer of ≥ 1:80 is considered highly clinically significant. 
    *   **Hypergammaglobulinemia:** The patient’s initial total serum protein was 8.2 g/dL. In the context of "normal" albumin (usually 3.5–4.5 g/dL), a total protein > 8.0 g/dL points to an elevated serum globulin fraction (> 3.5 g/dL). This is a hallmark of Autoimmune Hepatitis (primarily driven by intensely elevated IgG).
