## Phase 2 — Adversarial Self-Critique (skeptical second opinion)

### Strongest argument *against* Evans syndrome as the unifying diagnosis
The case includes features that could indicate a **thrombotic microangiopathy (TTP)** rather than immune cytopenias: **headache and intermittent blurred/double vision**, anemia + thrombocytopenia, and an episode of **hypotension**. TTP can have minimal early schistocytosis and can be missed if one anchors on a positive DAT.

### Findings not fully explained by the leading diagnosis
- **Neurologic/visual symptoms**: Evans/AIHA can cause nonspecific symptoms from anemia, but true focal/episodic diplopia raises concern for TTP or other CNS process.
- **Marked leukocytosis** (WBC 27k with left shift) and **very high ESR**: could be steroid effect/stress/inflammation, but it is not classic for isolated Evans.
- **Rising transaminases** later (ALT 349, AST 138) with **anti–smooth muscle antibody positivity**: suggests an autoimmune hepatitis process or viral hepatitis reactivation/flare, which is related but not “explained” by Evans alone.
- **Vesicular lesions (HSV-2)**: may be incidental or a trigger, but not intrinsic to Evans.

### Alternative diagnosis that could explain the constellation better
- **TTP (acquired ADAMTS13 deficiency)** could explain anemia + thrombocytopenia + neurologic symptoms and could be rapidly fatal if untreated. However, the **lack of schistocytes** and the strong immune-hemolysis signature (spherocytes, strong DAT positivity, low haptoglobin with reticulocytosis, indirect hyperbilirubinemia) weigh against TTP as primary.
- Another coherent alternative within immune hemolysis is **paroxysmal cold hemoglobinuria (Donath–Landsteiner hemolysis)** after a viral syndrome (hemoglobinuria/dark urine, complement-mediated DAT). But it is uncommon in adults and does not naturally explain the immune thrombocytopenia pattern.

### “Can’t miss” diagnosis I may be anchoring away from
**TTP** is the key “can’t miss.” If suspected clinically, it warrants **urgent ADAMTS13 testing and empiric plasma exchange** even before confirmation, because waiting can be catastrophic.
